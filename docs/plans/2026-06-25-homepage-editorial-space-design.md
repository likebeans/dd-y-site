# Homepage Editorial Space Design

## Goal

Turn the homepage from a portfolio-first landing page into a technical editorial space that still gives interviewers fast proof of project depth.

## Direction

The homepage should feel like the front door to a personal technical ecosystem:

- `yufeifan.cn` is the main editorial and professional identity.
- `notes.yufeifan.cn` will become the structured LLM knowledge base.
- `resume.yufeifan.cn` will become the interactive resume and portfolio lab.
- `/resume` remains the formal printable resume.

## Homepage Order

1. Hero: technical thesis, writing-first CTA, systems CTA, resume CTA.
2. Writing Tracks: long-term topics like RAG, Agent Runtime, Evaluation, Production Engineering.
3. Featured Writing: selected articles as proof of thinking.
4. Open Spaces: Notes on LLMs, Resume Lab, GitHub/Open Source.
5. Selected Work: systems proof.
6. Technical Focus: capability matrix.
7. Experience: compact professional proof.

## UI/UX Principles

- Keep the paper, ink, fine-line, rare-lime visual language.
- Avoid turning external spaces into large marketing cards.
- Use editorial rows, labels, and restrained borders.
- Give technical peers a reason to read further before asking them to view a resume.
- Preserve strong keyboard focus and mobile readability.

## Non-Goals

- Do not redesign Notes on LLMs or OpenResume yet.
- Do not add path-level reverse proxy.
- Do not add heavy animation or decorative gradients.
